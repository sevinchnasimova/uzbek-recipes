Here you will find recipes from Uzbekistan and other parts of Central Asia. I hope to share these dishes that I grew up eating and their cultural significance to Central Asia.

Introduction

Uzbek food is a combination of medieval Persian cuisine and nomadic Turkic elements. It emerged as a result of the coexistence and mixing of multiple cultures.

Uzbek food is meat-heavy, but there are dishes that everyone can love

Tea is the drink of Uzbek hospitality. It is offered to every guest and part of every meal.

Bread

Uzbek bread, or non, is eaten with almost everything.

Every region has it's own style of non and they are often baked in a hot clay oven known as tandyr. There is becomes golden and crispy.

Traditionally, Uzbek bread is not cut with a knife, but broken with hands.

Plov

The signature Uzbek dish is plov.

This main course is typically made with rice, pieces of meat and grated carrots and onions.

Chickpeas, raisins, barberries, and or fruit might be added for variation and extra flavor.
